# HydroAgent-Lab Visual Language

This document defines the visual direction for HydroAgent-Lab websites and future product pages. It converts the MotionSites-style prompt-library workflow into a practical house style for our brand.

## Design Position

HydroAgent-Lab should feel like a serious water-intelligence company with a cinematic first impression.

The site should not feel like:

- an academic lab page
- a generic SaaS template
- a dashboard product demo
- a dark cyberpunk AI startup
- a pile of rounded feature cards

The site should feel like:

- operationally credible
- visually spacious but information-dense
- modern, calm, and premium
- cinematic without becoming decorative
- designed for public customers, agencies, technical partners, and research collaborators

## Reference Method

Use MotionSites as a workflow reference, not as a source to copy directly.

The useful method is:

1. Pick 3-5 strong hero references before designing.
2. Extract the visual grammar: type scale, image treatment, density, motion, spacing, and CTA behavior.
3. Write one precise design prompt before touching code.
4. Generate or design the hero direction first.
5. Extend the same visual language into every page.
6. Review one page at a time instead of changing the whole site blindly.

## HydroAgent House Style

### Theme

Dark editorial with hydrologic atmosphere.

Use:

- deep blue-black or wet graphite backgrounds
- full-bleed environmental or control-room imagery
- thin technical dividers
- large white typography
- restrained cyan / green-blue accents
- high contrast but not neon

Avoid:

- purple-blue AI gradients as the main identity
- decorative blobs or glowing orbs
- excessive glassmorphism
- repeated card grids
- tiny gray labels everywhere

### Typography

Use typography as the main design element.

Hero headline:

- short
- large
- direct
- 1-3 lines
- confident, not explanatory

Good Chinese headline examples:

- 让水务决策更清晰。
- 水务智能，进入 Agentic 时代。
- 为业务预报构建 AI 操作层。
- 从预报到发布，一条清晰链路。

Good English headline examples:

- Water decisions, made clearer.
- Agentic intelligence for water operations.
- AI-native systems for operational hydrology.
- From forecast to release, one clear chain.

Avoid:

- long institutional sentences
- research-paper wording
- headlines that explain every capability
- mixed-size Chinese lines that break awkwardly

### Layout

Default page rhythm:

1. Full-bleed cinematic hero
2. One positioning statement
3. Architecture or operating model
4. Product / service map
5. Proof, team, or adoption path
6. Minimal CTA

Use:

- full-width sections
- wide content canvas
- large editorial split layouts
- rows and dividers for conceptual content
- cards only for actual objects

Avoid:

- narrow centered website shells
- floating nav boxes that make the page feel small
- many equal-height cards
- large empty whitespace without visual purpose
- section after section with the same pattern

### Imagery

The primary visual should show a credible water-intelligence world.

Use:

- hydrologic control room
- watershed / river-basin context
- forecasting map wall
- operational team reviewing data
- cinematic but realistic water-infrastructure scene

Image treatment:

- full-bleed or very large
- darkened only enough for text legibility
- avoid making the image muddy
- use subtle directional scrims instead of heavy black overlays

Avoid:

- small hero images inside frames
- generic abstract AI waves
- cartoon illustrations
- stock photos that do not reveal water, forecast, operation, or decision context

### Components

Navigation:

- fixed full-width bar
- low visual noise
- active state as line or subtle text change
- CTA visible but restrained

Sections:

- use dividers and columns before cards
- use large headings and compact explanations
- avoid too many small labels

Cards:

- allowed for products, team members, case studies, articles, or actual product modules
- banned as default wrappers for every idea

CTA:

- one primary action
- one optional secondary action
- no aggressive sales language

## Motion Direction

Motion should feel like operational clarity, not entertainment.

Use:

- subtle reveal on section entry
- slow image parallax only if performance stays smooth
- hover states that clarify clickability
- no delayed first-paint layering

Avoid:

- 1-2 second hero composition flash
- loading sequences that show visual layers popping in
- animated blobs or constant motion
- scroll-jacking

## HydroAgent Visual Prompt

Use this as the default visual-generation prompt:

```text
Create a premium, cinematic hero section for HydroAgent-Lab, an AI-native water-intelligence company serving forecasting agencies, water utilities, and research partners.

Visual style: dark editorial, high-trust enterprise technology, realistic hydrologic operations, full-bleed image, wide cinematic composition, large confident typography, restrained cyan/green-blue accent, thin technical lines, minimal chrome, no generic SaaS cards.

Scene: a modern water forecasting operations room overlooking a river basin or reservoir system, with analysts reviewing hydrologic maps, rainfall radar, river networks, forecast curves, and release decision context on large wall displays.

Mood: calm, serious, capable, premium, operationally credible. Not cyberpunk, not academic, not decorative, not cartoonish.

Layout: full-width fixed navigation, large left-aligned headline over the image, concise supporting copy, one primary CTA, one secondary CTA. Keep the hero image large and readable.
```

## Page Review Checklist

For every page, verify:

- Can the visitor understand HydroAgent-Lab within 5 seconds?
- Does the page look like a serious company site, not a generated template?
- Is the visual world consistent with water intelligence?
- Are there fewer cards than rows/dividers?
- Are labels large enough or removed?
- Does the Chinese copy sound public-facing?
- Does the English copy sound native?
- Is the first viewport strong without waiting for effects?
- Is the navigation full-width and stable?
- Does mobile preserve the same premium feeling?
